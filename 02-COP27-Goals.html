COP27 Goals

Introduction

There is vital work that needs to be done with governments and international organizations to make sure they remain committed to the Paris Agreement and the Glasgow Climate Pact, ensuring that we capitalize on momentum leading into COP27.

Our aim is to address the following:

  1. Ensure progress towards the Global Goal on Adaptation and fulfilling commitments towards doubling adaptation pledges. This will require working with all parties, international organizations to collectively achieve meaningful progress.
  2. Guarantee raising ambition to limit global warming to 1.5 degrees and ensure countries fulfil their commitments to update Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) and its potential support.
  3. Scaling up climate finance through working with all financial institutions and the private sector to ensure fulfilling pledges made by the COP Presidency.
  4. The meaningful participation of civil society, youth and women in dialogues and decision-making is paramount to successfully counter the adverse effects of climate change. This inclusive approach is a priority for advancing climate solutions and innovations, especially for the strong youth demographic across the African continent.

Mitigation

We must all unite to limit the global warming of the planet to 1.5C. This requires bold and immediate actions from all parties to ensure a sustainable livelihood for everyone on the planet.

COP27 will be a moment for countries to fulfil their commitments towards their people and to show ambition to curb the earthâ€™s rising temperature through support, technology, capacity building and more importantly the utmost solidarity, cooperation and collaboration.

Adaptation

The onset of extreme weather events from heatwaves, floods, forest fires have become an everyday reality of our lives.

World leaders, governments and state parties to the convention have called upon a Global Action Towards Adaptation in the COP26.

The Global Goal on Adaptation is a significantly critical milestone in COP27 and onwards to COP28, to urge all parties to adopt immediate actions to adapt to the impacts of climate change and save the most vulnerable communities.

Finance

From COP26 to COP27, it is essential for all stakeholders to ensure full participation and fulfil the pledges announced in Glasgow.

Moving from pledges to providing actual finance and access is paramount to guarantee the implementation of the mitigation and adaptation efforts.

The progress on the USD 100 billion will build more trust between developed and developing countries, both showing actual commitments being fulfilled and bring more ambition to COP27.

Collaboration

Enhancing and facilitating agreement in the negotiations is of the utmost importance for the Presidency of CO27 to achieve tangible results in a balanced manner. The advancement of partnership and collaboration will help deliver our four goals and ensure the world is adopting more a resilient, and sustainable economic model where humans are at the center of climate talks.

The UN negotiations are consensus-based, and reaching agreement will require inclusive and active participation from all stakeholders.

We are working tirelessly to ensure adequate representation and participation from all relevant stakeholder in COP27, especially vulnerable communities and representatives from countries in the African region whom are increasingly affected by the impacts of climate change.

We need to turn the Glasgow outcome into action, and commence with its implementation.

Ensuring humans are at the center of climate talks is imperative.

Governments, the private sector and civil society need to work, in tandem, to transform the way in which we interact with our planet. We must introduce new solutions and innovations that help alleviate the adverse impacts of climate change. We also need to replicate and rapidly upscale all other climate-friendly solutions towards implementation in developing countries.
